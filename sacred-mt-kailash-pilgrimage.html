Overview

What this retreat is

A pilgrimage where meditation meets adventure. Fourteen days circling the most sacred mountain on earth - Mt Kailash, abode of Shiva, source of the four great rivers of Asia, and the stillness at the centre of the spinning world.

Days of approach through the Tibetan plateau, three days of Kora circumambulation (52km, crossing the 5630m Drolma La pass), and integration days where the silence of the mountain integrates into the body. Held by Sarita, Swami Amandeep and Antar Rahi.

Common questions

FAQ

How fit do I need to be?

Reasonable physical fitness for high-altitude trekking is required. The Drolma La pass at 5630m is the hardest single day.

What about altitude sickness?

Real risk. We acclimatize for 3 days before the Kora. Medical clearance is required from your doctor.

Do I need to be a Buddhist or Hindu?

No. Mt Kailash is sacred to four traditions and welcomes any seeker who comes with respect and openness.

What gear do I need?

We send a complete packing list with the application.

Who this is for — and who it is not

This is for you if...

  • Practitioners ready for a pilgrimage, not a tour
  • Anyone with reasonable hiking fitness (3-5 hours/day at altitude)
  • Seekers of any tradition (Hindu, Buddhist, Jain, Bon, secular)
  • Those willing to commit to 90 days of altitude prep before departure
  • Both first-time pilgrims and returning ones — the mountain meets each differently

This may not be for you if...

  • Anyone with cardiac, respiratory, or blood pressure conditions without medical clearance
  • First-trimester pregnancy — altitude is unsafe
  • Anyone seeking comfort or luxury — this is the opposite
  • Those unable to commit to 14 days fully off-grid (no reliable signal)
  • Anyone expecting the trek to be the spiritual experience — the trek is the prep
A felt narrative

Fourteen days around the most sacred mountain on earth

The pilgrimage begins in Lhasa or Kathmandu, depending on permits. The first three days are acclimatization — sacred sites, low-altitude practice, rest. The body needs the time. Skipping this kills the trek.

Days four to six cross the Tibetan plateau. The land becomes vast in a way the European or American eye has rarely seen. The group quiets. We stop for prayer at sacred lakes. Sleep in basic guesthouses. Eat simple Tibetan food.

Days seven, eight, nine are the Kora itself — 52km of circumambulation around Mt Kailash, abode of Shiva. Day eight crosses the Drolma La pass at 5,630m. It is the hardest physical day of most participants' lives. It is also the day something that needed to break finally breaks.

Days ten to fourteen integrate. Sacred sites near Kailash. Silent walking. Daily teachings. By day thirteen, you understand why this mountain has called pilgrims of four traditions for thousands of years. Closing puja on day fourteen, then the long journey home — with a different body and a different inner geography.
