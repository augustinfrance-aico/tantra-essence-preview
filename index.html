A short word about Tantra

Tantra is not what you think.

Tantra is the oldest spiritual path on Earth — a 5,000-year-old science of awakening that uses every part of life as a doorway to the divine. Yes, including the body. Yes, including pleasure. Yes, including relationships. But also breath, meditation, devotion, sound, silence.

It is a path of embodied awakening — not retreat from the world, but a deeper plunge into it. With awareness as the only compass.

Common Questions

You're not alone in wondering

Real answers to the questions thousands of seekers have asked before walking this path.

Is Tantra Essence right for beginners?

Absolutely. Every program and retreat is designed with beginners in mind, with clear progression. Sarita guides you from the very first breath. No previous Tantra, meditation, or yoga experience required.

Do I have to be in a relationship to practice?

No. Tantra is first and foremost a relationship with yourself. Most of our programs are designed for solo practice. Some include optional partner exercises for couples who wish to grow together.

Is Tantra Essence connected to a religious tradition?

Tantra Essence is rooted in the Osho lineage — Sarita spent 17 years at Osho's side. The teaching is non-religious, non-dogmatic, and welcomes seekers from every background, faith, or absence of faith.

What's the difference between an online program and a retreat?

Online programs are self-paced, lifetime-access courses for daily practice at home. Retreats are immersive 7 to 34-day in-person experiences in sacred locations (Bali, Switzerland, India, USA), with Sarita and the Kaula faculty.

How much time per week should I commit?

2 to 4 hours per week is the sweet spot for most students. The practices are designed to weave into your real life, not replace it. You go at your own rhythm — there is no race.

Is there a money-back guarantee?

Yes. Every online program comes with a 14-day money-back guarantee. Try it, feel it, and if it's not the right fit, write to us and we'll refund you — no questions asked.

Is this safe for people with trauma history?

Yes, and we take this seriously. Our approach is trauma-informed, consent-led, and entirely paced by you. We also recommend pairing the work with a qualified therapist if you're processing significant past trauma.

Still have questions? Reach our support team directly or use the chat button on the bottom right.
